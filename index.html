Natural Ingredients Used In iGenics™
Bilberry

Often compared to the blueberry, the Bilberry is a potent source of anthocyanins—intense antioxidants specifically linked to eye health. It acts as a biological shield, helping to defend delicate ocular structures from the degenerative effects of free radicals and environmental oxidative stress. By supporting the regeneration of rhodopsin, it also plays a crucial role in improving night vision and helping the eyes adapt more quickly to low-light conditions.

Ginkgo Biloba

A celebrated botanical with a long history in traditional wellness, Ginkgo Biloba is prized for its ability to enhance blood flow and cognitive performance. Within this blend, it serves a critical purpose by optimizing microcirculation to the ocular tissues. By ensuring a consistent delivery of oxygen and life-sustaining nutrients to the eyes, it helps maintain the structural integrity and sharpness of your vision.

Turmeric & Bioperine

Turmeric is famous for its active component, curcumin, which offers profound anti-inflammatory benefits. In this formula, Turmeric works to mitigate inflammation, a primary culprit in many vision-related concerns. To maximize these effects, Bioperine—a refined black pepper extract—is included to significantly boost curcumin’s absorption, ensuring your body utilizes every milligram efficiently.

Saffron

Harvested from the Crocus sativus flower, Saffron is a precious spice with a legacy of therapeutic use. Its high concentration of specialized antioxidants provides targeted protection for the lens and retina, neutralizing inflammation and safeguarding the eyes against long-term oxidative wear. Clinical research suggests that regular intake of saffron can significantly enhance retinal sensitivity, providing a natural defense against the gradual decline of visual sharpness over time.

Zeaxanthin

As a foundational nutrient located within the macula, Zeaxanthin is vital for high-definition, detailed sight. It functions as an internal "light filter," working with other carotenoids to absorb harmful UV rays and blue light from digital devices. This defense mechanism helps lower the risk of age-related macular changes, sharpens contrast, and minimizes the fatigue often caused by prolonged screen use.
